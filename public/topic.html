HISTORY OF CIBERSECURITY (personal interest topic)

ARPANET (1969): The first network created so the military could communicate. It was set up between university nodes and funded by the U.S. Department of Defense. It later evolved into the Internet.

DATES:

  • 1971 The first non-malicious computer virus appeared, called Creeper. As a result, the first antivirus software was created.
  • 1978 The first spam incident occurred: an email was sent to promote a company’s product.
  • 1986 The Brain virus appeared, created to protect the rights of medical software, despite the existence of pirated copies of legally owned software.
  • 1988 The first massive virus outbreak occurred with the Morris Worm, created unintentionally by a student. It infected around 6,000 computers, caused losses of about $96 million, and led to a sentence of three years of probation.
  • 1996 Internet access began to be offered on a massive scale. Phone calls would interrupt the connection because both used the same frequency.
  • 2000 Hundreds of millions of users were already connected to the Internet, and the ILOVEYOU worm affected around 50 million computers through malicious email attachments, pushing governments and companies to take cybersecurity seriously.
  • + 2010 Cyber threats escalated significantly with advanced attacks such as Stuxnet, which damaged Iranian nuclear centrifuges, and the rapid growth of ransomware, including WannaCry and NotPetya in 2017, leading to widespread public, corporate, and governmental cybersecurity awareness.
