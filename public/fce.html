Fundamentals of Computer Engineering

Learning Objectives

The course aims for students to:

  • Understand the purpose and social impact of Computer Engineering: what is the ultimate goal of the discipline? and its contribution to societal well-being, fostering in students a sense of purpose, future role as an engineer.
  • Value the human factor and the vocation to serve: promoting a service-oriented ethic and a commitment to the common good.
  • Acquire a historical perspective on technological development, and the fundamental principles that drive innovation (tech progress).
  • Explore the professional landscape of Computer Engineering: understanding of the various fields, the different professional profiles (career paths).
  • Current paradigms and trends: contemporary computing models, data management, and an introduction to web development.

Parts of the Class

PART I - THEORETICAL FOUNDATIONS (10 units)

Block 1 - Computer Engineering in Society

  • Background and historical perspectives.
  • Basic definitions and core concepts.
  • The past, present, and future of ICT Computer Engineering.
  • Competencies and knowledge required of computer engineers.
  • The role of ethics in the education of computer engineers. Deontological codes.

Block 2 - Current Paradigms and Trends in Systems and Applications

  • Software development: more than just programming.
  • Structure of computer systems:
    • Hardware.
    • Software.
    • OOSS.
    • Networks.
  • Emerging paradigms and trends.

Block 3 - Information and Data

  • Representation of information in digital format.
    • Number systems.
    • Numerical and Alphanumeric data representation. Data foundations.
    • Big-Data foundations.
  • Laws and ethics.

PART II - PRACTICAL SKILL (4 units)

Block 1 - Introduction to Software Development

  • Introduction to web development.
  • Software repositories: Git and GitHub.

Block 2 - Creating Web Pages with HTML5

  • Basic concepts.
  • HTTP tags.
  • Organizing and presenting information.

Block 3 - Design and Layout with CSS3

  • Basic concepts.
  • Selectors.
  • Box model and positioning of elements.
